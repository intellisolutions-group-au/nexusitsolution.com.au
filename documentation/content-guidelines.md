# Content & Brand Guidelines - Nexus IT Solution

## 1. Brand Identity Overview
**Nexus IT Solution** positions itself as a premium, reliable, and forward-thinking technology partner for Australian businesses. We focus on bridging the gap between complex technology and business success.

## 2. Voice and Tone
- **Professional & Authoritative:** We know our stuff. We use industry-standard terminology but explain it clearly.
- **Supportive & Trustworthy:** IT issues can be stressful for businesses. Our tone should convey reliability and peace of mind.
- **Innovative:** We use modern solutions (like Next.js, Cloud architectures) and our language should reflect that.
- **Avoid:** Jargon overload, overly casual slang, negative phrasing.

## 3. Formatting Rules
- **Headings:** Use Sentence case for all H1, H2, H3 (e.g., "Managed IT services for modern businesses").
- **Bullet Points:** Keep them concise. Start with an action verb when outlining services or benefits.
- **Calls to Action (CTAs):** Use strong, clear commands.
  - *Good:* "Get Your Free IT Audit", "Contact Our Experts", "View Our Case Studies"
  - *Avoid:* "Click Here", "Submit"

## 4. Key Terminology
| Term to Use | Terms to Avoid |
|-------------|----------------|
| Custom Web Development | Web Design / Making Websites |
| Cyber Security | Hacking Protection |
| Digital Solutions | Computer Stuff |
| Cloud Infrastructure | Cloud Hosting |

## 5. Visual Asset Guidelines
- **Images:** High-quality, professional imagery showing diverse teams, modern office spaces, server racks, and abstract tech backgrounds. Avoid cliché stock photos (e.g., a person in a hoodie typing on a glowing keyboard).
- **Icons:** Use the Lucide React icon set for consistency. Minimal, line-based, scalable.
- **Color Usage:**
  - Primary text: `#0f172a` (Slate 900)
  - Action items/Links: `#3b82f6` (Blue 500)
  - Backgrounds: Use ample whitespace, `#ffffff` and `#f8fafc`.

## 6. Local Context (Australia)
- Ensure all spelling follows Australian/British English conventions (e.g., "Optimise" instead of "Optimize", "Programme" for non-computer contexts, though "Program" is fine for software).
- Use local references where appropriate (e.g., "Sydney-based team").
- Format phone numbers properly: `+61 2 XXXX XXXX` or `1300 XXX XXX`.
